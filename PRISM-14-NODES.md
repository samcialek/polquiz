# PRISM — 14 Node Reference

All continuous nodes use a 1-5 scale. EPS and AES are categorical. Each node also has a **salience** (0-5) indicating how much it matters to the archetype's identity.

Source: `nodes.ts` (verified 2026-03-28)

---

## ENDS — What you want

| Node | Name | What it measures | 1 (Left Pole) | 5 (Right Pole) |
|------|------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| **MAT** | Materialism | Economic orientation toward redistribution vs. markets | Redistributionist — state should actively level material outcomes | Market-oriented — markets allocate resources more efficiently |
| **CD** | Creative Destruction | Openness to cultural change and disruption | Embraces cultural change, cosmopolitan mixing, disruption of tradition | Preserves existing cultural structures, resists rapid change, values continuity |
| **CU** | Cultural Unity | Attitude toward cultural assimilation vs. pluralism | Assimilationist — shared civic culture is essential for cohesion | Pluralist — multiple cultural identities can coexist without a dominant frame |
| **MOR** | Moral Circle | Scope of moral concern | Particularist — moral obligations strongest toward in-group | Universalist — moral obligations extend equally to all humans |

## MEANS — How to achieve it

| Node | Name | What it measures | 1 (Left Pole) | 5 (Right Pole) |
|------|------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| **PRO** | Proceduralism | Whether process or outcome matters more | Outcome-focused — the right result justifies flexible means | Rules-bound — fair process matters even if the outcome is imperfect |
| **EPS** | Epistemology | How you know what's true | *Categorical: intuitionist / empiricist / rationalist / autonomous* | |
| **AES** | Political Aesthetic | Style of political engagement | *Categorical: statesman / visionary / guardian / prophet* | |
| **COM** | Compromise | Willingness to make deals and seek consensus | Uncompromising/principled — holds the line on values | Compromising/consensus-seeking — pragmatic deal-making |

## REALITY — How you see the world

| Node | Name | What it measures | 1 (Left Pole) | 5 (Right Pole) |
|------|------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| **ZS** | Zero-Sum | View of whether gains require others' losses | Positive-sum — cooperation expands the pie for everyone | Zero-sum — gains for one group come at another's expense |
| **ONT_H** | Ontology — Human Nature | Belief about whether people are fundamentally good or flawed | Pessimistic — people are self-interested and need constraints | Optimistic — people are fundamentally cooperative and trustworthy |
| **ONT_S** | Ontology — System | Belief about whether current institutions work | System works — institutions are broadly functional and improvable | System broken — institutions are captured, corrupt, or fundamentally failing |

## SELF — Your political identity

| Node | Name | What it measures | 1 (Left Pole) | 5 (Right Pole) |
|------|------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| **PF** | Partisan Identity | Strength of partisan attachment | Independent/non-partisan — evaluates outside party frames | Strong partisan identity — deeply identified with a party |
| **TRB** | Tribalism | Strength of in-group political identity | Low tribal — evaluates issues on merit, weak group loyalty | High tribal — strong in-group loyalty, politics as team sport |
| **ENG** | Engagement | Level of political participation and attention | Disengaged — rarely follows politics, low participation | Highly engaged — follows politics closely, votes, participates actively |
