ARCHETYPE #001
Rawlsian Reformer

A principled egalitarian who insists on fair procedures and universal moral standards. Deeply pluralist and institutionalist in style, they reject zero-sum thinking and tribal politics. Approaches reform through rules-based statecraft, never compromising on the idea that justice requires both redistribution and procedural integrity.

ARCHETYPE #011
Jacobin Egalitarian

A fiery egalitarian who sees politics as zero-sum class struggle. Uncompromising, outcome-focused, and deeply culturally progressive. Relies on intuitionist epistemology and the fighter aesthetic, rejecting statesmanship and technocracy as complicit moderation. Highly engaged, with a revolutionary sense of urgency.

ARCHETYPE #056
Institutional Leftist

An institutional centrist who combines traditional cultural leanings with deep pluralism and fierce anti-zero-sum conviction. System-trusting, anti-tribal, and engaged. Institutionalist in epistemology and statesman in style — believes existing institutions, properly maintained, can accommodate diversity without sacrificing stability.

ARCHETYPE #088
Gentle Traditionalist

A gentle traditionalist who combines cultural conservatism with genuine openness to compromise and pastoral warmth. Assimilationist but not combative, traditionalist-pastoral in epistemology and aesthetics, system-critical yet temperamentally moderate. Values continuity and community feeling over ideological confrontation or political engagement.
