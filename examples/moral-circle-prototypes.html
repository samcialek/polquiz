Moral Circle as a 7-Section Radial

Premise. Collapse Partisan Fusion and Tribalism into a single richer Moral Circle node with seven sub-categories. Each sub-category has its own position (how much you treat that group as your in-group, 0–100%) and salience (how tribally you hold it, 0–3). The 7 categories: national, ethnic / racial, religious, class, ideological, gender, political tribe.

Visual rule. The 50% ring is the boundary between in-group and out-group. Sections at >50% spike out beyond the ring; sections at <50% stay within. Salience controls visual weight (saturation, thickness, or vertex size depending on the prototype).

Four visualization prototypes · same data, different shape
Sample respondent:
Prototype A
Sunburst Spike
Pie wedges that erupt past the 50% ring

Each of 7 wedges fills its angular slice from center to its position. The dashed ring is 50%. Wedges with position >50% extend past the ring; wedges <50% stay inside. Salience drives saturation — high-salience wedges read as bold, low-salience as washed out. Compact and structural; reads at glance as "shape of who they include."

Prototype B
Radial Bars
Thin spokes whose thickness encodes salience

Seven bars radiate from center. Length = position, thickness = salience (a thin wisp at sal=0, a chunky bar at sal=3). One color per category. Cleaner separation between adjacent categories than the wedge prototype; easier to read individual bars but loses the "filled territory" gestalt.

Prototype C
Spider Polygon
Connected vertices over a 50% reference ring

Classic radar chart. Seven axes with 25/50/75% grid rings. Vertices placed at each category's position; the polygon connecting them is filled translucent. Vertex dot size = salience. Familiar to most readers (radar charts are widespread); shows the overall "shape" of someone's moral circle as a single connected form.

Prototype D
Petal Bloom
Teardrop petals; organic, less data-viz feel

Each category is a teardrop petal. Length = position, base width = salience. Most aesthetic; reads as an organic bloom rather than an analytic chart. Trade-off: harder to compare individual values precisely, easier to remember as a visual signature.
