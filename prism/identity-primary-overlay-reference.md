# Identity-Primary Overlay Reference (Compact)

## Architecture
- **Layer type:** Overlay/modifier on existing archetypes
- **Gate:** High TRB + High PF + Q60 identity ranking + demographics (disambiguator only)
- **ENG** is the activation factor, not a gate node — determines latent/active/dominant state
- **Modifies:** Election history, coalition alignment, world map, narrative
- **Does NOT modify:** Node scores, archetype classification
- **Three states:** Latent (identity exists, low activation) → Active (mobilized, affects choice) → Dominant (primary driver)
- **Three outputs:** No overlay / Resolved overlay / Unresolved identity-primary fit
- **Demographics rule:** Necessary disambiguator, never standalone assigner

## 1. The Black Voter

- **Applicability:** 1868–present (15th Amendment)
- **Suppression zone:** 1880–1964 (Jim Crow, Southern turnout near zero)
- **Coalition path:** R (1868–1932) → Transition (1932–1964) → **D locked (1964–present)**
- **Hinge elections:** 1936 (FDR flip, ~70% D), **1964 (Civil Rights Act, 94% D — the lock)**
- **Current status:** D, 86-90%. Slight erosion among Black men since 2016
- **Confidence:** Very high. Best-documented identity-primary bloc in American political science
- **Notes:** The 1964 lock has held for 60 years across every ideological configuration

## 2. The White Grievance Voter

- **Applicability:** 1828–present (Jacksonian populism as proto-form; identity-primary from ~1856)
- **Coalition path:** D solid South (1856–1948) → Transition (1948–1972) → **R locked (1972–present)**
- **Hinge elections:** 1948 (Dixiecrat revolt, first crack), **1964 (Goldwater carries Deep South)**, 1972 (Southern Strategy complete), **2016 (Trump = full activation)**
- **Current status:** R, fully activated. Trump IS this overlay's candidate
- **Confidence:** Very high post-1964. Medium pre-Civil War (hard to separate identity-primary from structural default)
- **Notes:** 1964 Mirror — same event (Civil Rights Act) locks Black voters D and breaks White Grievance from D

## 3. The Evangelical Voter

- **Applicability:** 1828–present, with 50-year dormancy (1928–1976)
- **Dormancy:** Post-Scopes fundamentalist retreat. Evangelicals exist but are NOT identity-primary voters
- **Coalition path:** Whig/R (1828–1928) → Dormant (1928–1976) → Carter drift (1976) → **R locked (1980–present)**
- **Hinge elections:** 1928 (anti-Smith = last pre-dormancy identity vote), 1976 (Carter drift — false positive), **1980 (Moral Majority + Reagan = the lock)**
- **Current status:** R, 76-81%. Passes the Trump Test — identity > ideology
- **Confidence:** Very high post-1980. Low during dormancy. Medium pre-Scopes (anachronistic category)
- **Notes:** Most ideologically "full" overlay — carries real doctrinal content (abortion, marriage). But Trump Test proves identity layer survives ideology collapse

## 4. The LGBTQ Voter

- **Applicability:** 1980–present (AIDS crisis makes it electorally legible)
- **N/A zone:** All elections before 1972. Proto 1972–1976
- **Coalition path:** Proto-D (1972–1976) → Emerging (1980) → **D locked (1984–present)**
- **Hinge elections:** **1984 (AIDS crisis locks D)**, 2004 (anti-gay marriage ballots = threat), 2015 (marriage equality won but anti-trans = new threat)
- **Current status:** D, 73-78%
- **Confidence:** High. Short history but consistent
- **Notes:** Shortest electoral history of any overlay (~45 years). Each person's identity is individually formed (not inherited), making the political attachment individually chosen but no less identity-primary

## 5. The Feminist Voter

- **Applicability:** 1980–present (gender gap first appears)
- **N/A zone:** 1789–1916 (no suffrage). 1920–1976 (women vote but NOT identity-primary — no gender gap)
- **Coalition path:** N/A → Proto-D (1932–1976, New Deal benefits women) → **D activated (1980–present)**
- **Hinge elections:** **1980 (ERA failure + Reagan = gender gap appears, 8 pts)**, 2016 (Clinton nomination, gap ~13 pts), **2024 (Dobbs = maximum threat activation)**
- **Current status:** D, widening gender gap. Dobbs is the most powerful activation event since 1980
- **Confidence:** High post-1980. Critical note: women voted slightly MORE Republican than men in some 1950s–60s elections. Feminist identity-primary is genuinely modern
- **Notes:** Women voting ≠ feminist identity-primary voting. The gap between 1920 (suffrage) and 1980 (gender gap) is 60 years

## 6. The Male Grievance Voter

- **Applicability:** 2016–present
- **N/A zone:** All elections before 2012. Proto 2012
- **Coalition path:** N/A → Proto (2012) → **R activated (2016–present)**
- **Hinge elections:** **2016 (Gamergate → manosphere → Trump = activation)**
- **Current status:** R, consolidating. Young male R shift accelerating 2020→2024
- **Confidence:** High on existence, medium on permanence. Only 3 elections of data
- **Notes:** Structurally mirrors the Feminist Voter, activated ~40 years later. May be unstable or may consolidate. The Tate/Peterson/Rogan ecosystem is the identity infrastructure

## Design Rules
1. An overlay can be **dormant** — group exists but isn't identity-primary electoral
2. An overlay can be **N/A** — group doesn't exist as a political identity yet
3. **Don't backproject** — LGBTQ before Stonewall is N/A, not "proto-D"
4. **Drift ≠ realignment** — Carter '76 evangelicals and Eisenhower Black voters are drifts, not transitions
5. **The Trump Test** — if a bloc holds coalition despite policy platform collapse, identity-primary confirmed
