# Identity-Primary Overlays — 60-Election Mapping (Three-State Model)

## Legend
- **State:** Latent / Active / Dominant
  - **Latent** — identity structure exists socially but below electoral activation threshold. Abstention likely, weak lean at best.
  - **Active** — identity is politically mobilized, affects coalition choice. Reliable lean, shows up.
  - **Dominant** — identity is THE primary driver. Very sticky. Won't defect without threat inversion.
- **Alignment:** R = Republican, D = Democratic, DR = Democratic-Republican, W = Whig, F = Federalist, Split = divided
- **Confidence:** VH = very high, H = high, M = medium, L = low

## Confidence Notes

### Highest confidence
- Black 1964 lock (VH) — best-documented identity-primary shift
- White Grievance 1964-1972 transition (VH) — mirror
- Evangelical 1980 lock (VH) — Moral Majority well-documented
- All overlays modern era (1980-2024) — polling data exists

### Lowest confidence
- White Grievance pre-Civil War: identity-primary vs structural default hard to separate
- Evangelical pre-Scopes: "evangelical" as category is anachronistic
- Male Grievance: 3 elections. Flash or permanent?
- All overlays in latency: by definition, latent = low confidence about alignment
- LGBTQ 1972-1980: emerging phase is inherently fuzzy

### Key conceptual gains from three-state model
1. **No more fake N/A** — identity exists even when electorally invisible
2. **Dormancy is latency, not absence** — Evangelical 1928-1976 is the proof case
3. **ENG mediates activation** — same identity, different ENG, different electoral behavior
4. **Male Grievance skipping "active"** is now visible as a distinct pattern, not just "appeared from nothing"
5. **Suppression ≠ non-existence** — Black voter 1880-1964 South is latent (forced), not absent
