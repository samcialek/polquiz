How to read these gaps — vote share is a turnout-conditional comparison

Important: the "Pred D / Actual D" headline columns are shares among those who voted on each side — but the two voter pools are not the same population. The model abstains 0–5.5% of respondents across all cycles; reality abstains 35–42%. So the predicted D-share is effectively the D-lean of the entire CCES sample, while the actual D-share is the D-lean of the ~60% of eligible adults who actually showed up. Real voters are a higher-engagement, higher-information slice of the electorate — and that slice differs systematically from the full sample. The abstention-model gap (the rightmost group in the summary table) is the load-bearing untreated bias.

  • Abstention model is uncalibrated. The predictVote clearing-bar buckets (apolitical/casual/engaged/highly-engaged) plus the mapper's engagement-from-newsint-and-turnout signal together yield a near-zero abstain rate in every cycle. This is the single biggest separable error. A calibrated abstention layer would shrink the predicted voter pool to ~the same size as the actual voter pool, and the in-pool D/R gap would re-baseline.
  • 2008 (~5% mapper coverage): all node positions fall back to uniform priors. Predictions reflect the population center distance to each candidate, not modelled vote choice. The 31pp gap is a coverage-gap demonstration.
  • 2012 (~20% mapper coverage, MAT only): economic-axis signal only; cultural / moral / system axes are fallback. Directional read at best.
  • 2016 / 2020 / 2024: meaningful coverage. Three additive biases visible at the model layer:
    • Pre-existing model D-bias of ~11pp on 2020 archetype-centroid baseline (voteModelSmoke): centrist policy positions sit closer to D candidate profiles than to recent R candidates (especially Trump's CD/CU/MOR positioning).
    • Thin-coverage shrinkage adds ~10pp: respondents with mostly-fallback signatures land at the population center, which is closer to D issue positions.
    • partyID is not passed to the predictor — partisanLoyaltyMultiplier (post-1932 modifier) defaults to 1.0×, removing the partisan-loyalty pull that would route cross-pressured voters back to their party despite issue-distance.
  • Directionality: the model gets 4/5 winners right. 2024 (Trump win) is incorrectly predicted as D — same finding as the archetype-centroid baseline.
